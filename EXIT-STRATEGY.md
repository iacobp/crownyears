## Brand Voice — Calibrated for Exit

### The Voice: Vogue meets Net-a-Porter editorial meets Brene Brown

**We are NOT:** a blogger, a self-help guru, a menopause brand, a scrappy startup.
**We ARE:** an editorial authority, a premium resource, a movement with tools.

### Voice Rules

1. **State, don't ask.** "Here's what works." Not "Maybe you could try..."
2. **Direct warmth.** Like a brilliant friend who's 5 years ahead.
3. **Evidence over platitudes.** Numbers, named products, real data. Never vague "self-care."
4. **Permission, not advice.** "You're allowed to." Not "You should."
5. **Restraint.** Fewer words. More white space. Vogue doesn't explain — it presents.
6. **Never cringe.** No "girl boss", "slay queen", "you go girl", "bestie." She's beyond that.
7. **Assumes intelligence.** Never dumbs down. She runs companies and manages estates.
8. **Specificity is luxury.** "The 30 pieces" not "some outfit ideas." "$19/month" not "affordable."

### Headline Patterns

DO:
- "The 30 Pieces That Replace Your Entire Closet"
- "What Your Lawyer Won't Tell You About Gray Divorce"
- "Reinvention Is Not a Luxury. It's a Right."
- "The Complete Financial Guide to Divorcing After 50"
- "Why Your Second Act Will Be Your Best"

DON'T:
- "Cute Outfit Ideas for Women Over 50!"
- "10 Divorce Tips You Need to Know"
- "How to Start Over After Divorce (It's Easier Than You Think!)"
- "Self-Care Sunday Vibes for Midlife Queens"
