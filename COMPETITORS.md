## What the Best Competitors Do Well

### 1. Goop — Content-to-Commerce Machine
**The play:** Every article naturally leads to a purchase. Not with banner ads — with contextual recommendations embedded in the editorial. "Here's why magnesium matters for sleep" → "These are the 3 magnesium supplements our wellness team actually uses" → product links.

**Revenue model:** $350+ AOV, 1M email subscribers, podcast, events ($$$), DTC products.

**What we steal:** The seamless content → commerce flow. Our capsule wardrobe post should have affiliate links to every piece. Our travel guides should link to booking. Every article is a revenue opportunity disguised as editorial.

### 2. Boom! Beauty — One Article = $22M
**The play:** Ezra Firestone wrote one blog post: "5 Makeup Tips For Older Women." He ran Facebook ads to it. The article built trust. At the bottom: product. One article. $22 million in attributed sales.

**Revenue model:** Facebook ads → educational article → product page → purchase. Repeat.

**What we steal:** The Facebook → article → conversion funnel. We don't need 100 articles. We need 5 perfect articles with perfect funnels behind them. And Facebook ads targeting women 45-65 are CHEAP because nobody else is targeting them.

### 3. NOON — Community is the Moat
**The play:** Eleanor Mills didn't build a product. She built a tribe. "NOON Circles" — small groups of midlife women meeting regularly (online + IRL). Retreats. Events. The Substack newsletter has 52% open rates (industry average: 20%).

**Revenue model:** £6-£35/month membership, retreats (£200+), events, partnerships.

**What we steal:** The community-first approach. "Crown Circles" — our version. Newsletter-driven. 52% open rates prove this demo READS emails. Also: she sells NO products. That's our gap. We add commerce to her community model.

### 4. Fabulous50s — YouTube Owns This Demo
**The play:** 2.2M subscribers watching fitness content for women 50+. Schellea Fowler posts consistently, focuses on low-impact workouts, and has become the default fitness voice for this audience.

**Revenue model:** YouTube ad revenue, donations ("Buy Me a Tea"), website content.

**What we steal:** YouTube is massively underexploited by everyone except Fabulous50s. Our posts could become short YouTube videos. "The 30 Pieces That Replace Your Entire Closet" is a 10-minute video. "15 Solo Travel Destinations" is a series.

### 5. Stripes — PE Acquisition Playbook
**The play:** Naomi Watts founded it. L Catterton (LVMH's PE arm) acquired majority stake. Revenue 3x'd in one year. Now in Ulta and Credo Beauty nationwide. Partnered with Wisp telehealth.

**Revenue model:** DTC + retail. Beauty products $40-$85. Telehealth partnership.

**What we steal:** The acquisition blueprint. L Catterton looks for: authentic founder story + growing audience + expandable product line + clear demographic. Crown Years checks these boxes if we hit the right metrics. Also: their Wisp telehealth partnership = affiliate revenue model for us.

## Strategic Recommendations

### Immediate (This Month)
1. **Activate the Boom! funnel:** Write 2-3 more "perfect articles" with affiliate links baked in. Run $50-$100 Facebook ads targeting women 45-65. Measure article → click → conversion.
2. **Newsletter live NOW.** Goop has 1M subscribers. NOON has 52% open rates. Email is the #1 channel. Every day without capture is wasted traffic.
3. **Claim the "Crown Years" name everywhere:** Instagram, Pinterest, Facebook, YouTube, TikTok, Substack — even if we don't post yet. Brand protection.

### Short-term (Month 2-3)
4. **Programmatic SEO:** Build state-by-state divorce pages (50 pages from one template). Massive traffic play.
5. **First digital product:** The Divorce Money Map ($19). Test on Gumroad. Promote through the blog post funnel.
6. **YouTube pilot:** Turn the capsule wardrobe post and solo travel guide into 2 YouTube videos. Test this channel.

### Medium-term (Month 3-6)
7. **Facebook Group:** "Crown Years Community" — steal the NOON Circles concept. Private, moderated, high-quality discussion.
8. **Affiliate program:** Systematically add affiliate links to every existing post. Fashion (Nordstrom, Net-a-Porter), wellness (AG1, Oura), beauty (Trinny London), travel (Booking.com).
9. **Partnership outreach:** Pitch Midi Health, Stripes, Trinny London for co-branded content or affiliate deals.

### Long-term (Month 6-12)
10. **Crown Circles:** Small-group meetup format (online + IRL). This is the community moat that makes us acquirable.
11. **First retreat:** One event. Aspirational location. $999-$2,500. Creates scarcity and brand elevation.
12. **Premium membership:** $29/month. Exclusive content, workshops, Circle access.
