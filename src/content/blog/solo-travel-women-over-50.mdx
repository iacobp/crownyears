Solo female travel is a $363 billion market. The fastest-growing segment: women over 50.

Not because they've run out of people to travel with. Because they've finally realized that the best trip of their life might be the one where nobody else decides the itinerary.

This is the guide. Where to go. How to plan. What most guides won't tell you. And why women who travel alone at 50 almost universally say the same thing: *"I should have done this years ago."*

## Why Solo Travel Hits Different After 50

At 25, traveling alone is an adventure. At 50, it's something else entirely — a **reclamation**. You're not finding yourself. You're choosing yourself.

The women who travel solo after 50 report:

- **Higher confidence** in all areas of life after returning home
- **Better problem-solving skills** — navigating a foreign city alone transfers directly to navigating complex life decisions
- **Deeper friendships formed on the road** — solo travelers meet more people than couples do
- **Clarity on what they actually want** — hard to hear your own voice when someone else is always talking

> I spent 30 years traveling as someone's wife and someone's mother. My first solo trip to Lisbon at 53 was the first time I traveled as myself. I cried on the plane home — not because I was sad, but because I'd forgotten what my own company felt like. — Patricia, 56

## The 15 Destinations

### Tier 1: First Solo Trip (Safe, Easy, English-Friendly)

**1. Lisbon, Portugal**
Walkable, affordable, stunning, and deeply safe. The light alone is worth the flight. Pasteis de nata for breakfast, fado at night, tile-covered streets in between. The solo traveler's perfect first city.
**Best for:** First-time solo travelers. Architecture and food lovers.
**Budget:** $120-$200/day comfortable.
**Stay:** 5-7 days.

**2. Edinburgh, Scotland**
Compact, literary, endlessly atmospheric. Walk the Royal Mile, tour the whisky distilleries, eat at places that would cost three times as much in London. English-speaking with Scottish warmth.
**Best for:** Book club women. History and culture lovers.
**Budget:** $150-$250/day.
**Stay:** 4-6 days.

**3. Copenhagen, Denmark**
The safest city in Europe by most rankings. Cycling culture, world-class food scene, design everywhere you look. Danish hygge is essentially the national brand of self-care.
**Best for:** Design and food lovers. Those who value feeling safe above all.
**Budget:** $180-$300/day (it's Scandinavia).
**Stay:** 4-5 days.

**4. Victoria, British Columbia**
European charm without the jet lag. Butchart Gardens, afternoon tea at the Empress, whale watching. Walkable, safe, and you can pair it with Vancouver or a ferry to the San Juan Islands.
**Best for:** Nature lovers. Women who want "abroad" without a passport.
**Budget:** $150-$250/day.
**Stay:** 3-5 days.

### Tier 2: The Transformative Trips

**5. Tuscany, Italy (Cooking School Stay)**
Rent a villa or book a cooking school week. Not Florence (too crowded). The countryside — Montepulciano, Montalcino, Cortona. Learn to make pasta from a woman who's been doing it for 60 years. Come home changed.
**Best for:** Food lovers. Women processing a life transition.
**Budget:** $200-$400/day (villa + cooking school).
**Stay:** 7-10 days.

**6. Kyoto, Japan**
The world's most aesthetically perfect city. Temples, tea ceremonies, gardens designed over centuries, food that redefines what food can be. Japan is also the safest country for solo female travelers, period.
**Best for:** Aesthetic-driven travelers. Mindfulness seekers.
**Budget:** $150-$300/day.
**Stay:** 7-10 days.

**7. Provence, France**
Lavender fields, village markets, rose wine at 2pm because you can. Rent a car and drift between hilltop towns. This is the trip from the vision board — and it's exactly as good as you imagine.
**Best for:** The woman who needs permission to slow down.
**Budget:** $180-$350/day.
**Stay:** 7-14 days.

**8. Sedona, Arizona**
Red rocks, energy vortexes, world-class spas, and the kind of sunsets that make you question your life choices — in the best way. Deeply popular with solo women 45+. You'll meet them on every trail.
**Best for:** Wellness seekers. Women who need nature therapy.
**Budget:** $150-$300/day.
**Stay:** 4-7 days.

### Tier 3: The Bold Adventures

**9. The Camino de Santiago, Spain**
Walk. Think. Meet extraordinary people. Shed everything that isn't essential — literally and figuratively. Women over 50 are the fastest-growing segment of Camino walkers. You don't need to be religious. You need to be ready to move.
**Best for:** Women in transition. Those who process life through movement.
**Budget:** $50-$100/day (pilgrimage pricing).
**Stay:** 5-35 days depending on route.

**10. Morocco (Marrakech + Atlas Mountains)**
Intoxicating, overwhelming, beautiful. Best experienced with a guided day or two to orient yourself, then solo exploration. The riads (boutique guesthouses) are some of the most beautiful accommodation on Earth.
**Best for:** Experienced travelers seeking intensity.
**Budget:** $80-$200/day.
**Stay:** 7-10 days.

**11. Iceland (Ring Road)**
Rent a campervan. Drive the Ring Road. Geysers, waterfalls, black sand beaches, hot springs at midnight under summer light. Zero crime. The landscape will humble you in the best possible way.
**Best for:** Adventure seekers. Women who need perspective.
**Budget:** $200-$350/day.
**Stay:** 10-14 days.

### Tier 4: The Retreats

**12. Bali, Indonesia (Ubud)**
The Eat Pray Love effect was real — but the truth is better than the movie. Ubud offers world-class yoga, rice paddies, temple ceremonies, and a cost of living that lets you stay for weeks. The expat community of women 45+ is thriving.
**Best for:** Extended stays. Yoga and wellness.
**Budget:** $60-$150/day.
**Stay:** 14-30 days.

**13. Costa Rica (Nosara or Santa Teresa)**
Surf lessons at any age. Yoga every morning. Monkeys in the trees. Fresh ceviche at sunset. Safe, welcoming, English-friendly, and packed with solo women travelers.
**Best for:** Active travelers. Women who want sun and movement.
**Budget:** $100-$200/day.
**Stay:** 7-14 days.

**14. The Scottish Highlands**
For the introvert. Castle ruins, single-malt whisky, landscapes that haven't changed in 500 years. Rent a cottage. Read books. Walk for hours without seeing another person. Come home knowing what silence sounds like.
**Best for:** Introverts. Readers. Women recharging from caregiving.
**Budget:** $120-$250/day.
**Stay:** 5-10 days.

**15. Puglia, Italy**
Tuscany's less crowded, less expensive, equally beautiful sister. Trulli houses, olive groves, the best food in Italy (fight me), and a pace of life that makes you question everything about how you've been living.
**Best for:** Off-the-beaten-path seekers. Food lovers.
**Budget:** $100-$250/day.
**Stay:** 7-10 days.

## The Packing Principle

<Image src="/blog/travel-packing-flatlay.jpg" alt="Solo travel packing essentials — curated and minimal" width={2048} height={2048} className="w-full max-w-lg mx-auto rounded-2xl my-12" />

One carry-on. That's it. The art of solo travel packing:

- **3 bottoms** (dark jeans, comfortable pants, a skirt or shorts)
- **5 tops** (2 tees, 1 blouse, 1 sweater, 1 layer)
- **1 dress** that works for dinner
- **2 pairs of shoes** (comfortable walking shoes + sandals or flats)
- **1 scarf** (solves every styling problem and doubles as a blanket)
- **Neutral color palette** so everything mixes

The woman who packs light travels with confidence. The woman who packs heavy travels with anxiety.

## What Nobody Tells You

**You will eat alone. It will feel strange for exactly one meal.** By the second meal, you'll have a book, a glass of wine, and the realization that this is actually wonderful.

**You will get lost.** This is not a failure. This is how you find the best things.

**Someone will worry about you.** Let them. Their worry is not your responsibility.

**You will come home different.** Not dramatically. Quietly. The woman who walks back through her own front door is not the same woman who left. She's the one who knows she can.

> The world doesn't get smaller after 50. Your reasons for seeing it get better.

## Your First Step

Pick one city from the list. Not the boldest one. The one that made your stomach flutter slightly when you read it. Book a flight. Book three nights. That's it. Don't plan every day. Don't ask permission. Just go.

You've spent decades navigating things far more complex than a foreign city. You can handle this. And you'll wonder why you waited so long.
