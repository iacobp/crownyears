A solo trip offers one rare pleasure: complete authorship of the itinerary.

This guide covers where to go, how to plan, what to pack, and the small details that make a first solo trip feel easy enough to book.

## Why Solo Travel Feels Different After 50

At 25, traveling alone is an adventure. At 50, adventure comes with authorship. You choose the pace, the table, the view, and the hour you come home.

The women who travel solo after 50 report:

- **Higher confidence** in all areas of life after returning home
- **Better problem-solving skills:** navigating a foreign city alone transfers directly to complex life decisions
- **Deeper friendships formed on the road:** solo travelers often meet people at cafés, classes, tours, and shared tables
- **Clarity on what they want:** a quiet itinerary creates room to hear your own preferences again

## The 15 Destinations

### Tier 1: First Solo Trip (Safe, Easy, English-Friendly)

**1. Lisbon, Portugal**
Walkable, affordable, and full of beauty. The light alone is worth the flight. Pastéis de nata for breakfast, fado at night, tile-covered streets in between. Lisbon makes a generous first city.
**Choose it for:** A first solo trip, architecture, and food.
**Budget:** $120-$200/day comfortable.
**Stay:** 5-7 days.

**2. Edinburgh, Scotland**
Compact, literary, endlessly atmospheric. Walk the Royal Mile, tour the whisky distilleries, eat at places that would cost three times as much in London. English-speaking with Scottish warmth.
**Choose it for:** Books, history, and culture.
**Budget:** $150-$250/day.
**Stay:** 4-6 days.

**3. Copenhagen, Denmark**
Orderly streets, cycling culture, a serious food scene, and design everywhere you look. Danish hygge turns warmth and comfort into a civic art.
**Choose it for:** Design, food, and an easy rhythm.
**Budget:** $180-$300/day (it's Scandinavia).
**Stay:** 4-5 days.

**4. Victoria, British Columbia**
European charm close to home. Butchart Gardens, afternoon tea at the Empress, whale watching. Walkable, welcoming, and easy to pair with Vancouver or a ferry to the San Juan Islands.
**Choose it for:** Nature and a passport-free taste of Europe.
**Budget:** $150-$250/day.
**Stay:** 3-5 days.

### Tier 2: The Transformative Trips

**5. Tuscany, Italy (Cooking School Stay)**
Rent a villa or book a cooking school week in the countryside: Montepulciano, Montalcino, or Cortona. Learn to make pasta from a woman who has shaped it by hand for 60 years. Come home changed.
**Choose it for:** Food and the breathing room of a life transition.
**Budget:** $200-$400/day (villa + cooking school).
**Stay:** 7-10 days.

**6. Kyoto, Japan**
Kyoto is tuned to detail. Temples, tea ceremonies, gardens shaped over centuries, and food that rewards attention make each day feel composed.
**Choose it for:** Beauty, ritual, and quiet attention.
**Budget:** $150-$300/day.
**Stay:** 7-10 days.

**7. Provence, France**
Lavender fields, village markets, rosé at 2pm because you can. Rent a car and drift between hilltop towns. The trip from the vision board lives up to the picture.
**Choose it for:** Long lunches, village markets, and a slower clock.
**Budget:** $180-$350/day.
**Stay:** 7-14 days.

**8. Sedona, Arizona**
Red rocks, energy vortexes, world-class spas, and sunsets that rearrange your sense of scale. Solo women 45+ fill the trails, spa lounges, and sunrise lookouts.
**Choose it for:** Wellness, desert air, and perspective.
**Budget:** $150-$300/day.
**Stay:** 4-7 days.

### Tier 3: The Bold Adventures

**9. The Camino de Santiago, Spain**
Walk. Think. Meet extraordinary people. Shed the extra weight in your pack and your mind. Women over 50 are the fastest-growing segment of Camino walkers. The route welcomes every reason for walking. Readiness matters.
**Choose it for:** Life transitions and thinking through movement.
**Budget:** $50-$100/day (pilgrimage pricing).
**Stay:** 5-35 days depending on route.

**10. Morocco (Marrakech + Atlas Mountains)**
Intoxicating, overwhelming, beautiful. Begin with a guided day or two, then explore solo. A tiled courtyard and a quiet riad make a restorative counterpoint to the medina.
**Choose it for:** Intensity, craft, color, and an experienced traveler’s confidence.
**Budget:** $80-$200/day.
**Stay:** 7-10 days.

**11. Iceland (Ring Road)**
Rent a campervan. Drive the Ring Road. Geysers, waterfalls, black sand beaches, and hot springs at midnight under summer light. Iceland pairs a strong reputation for safety with a landscape that restores perspective.
**Choose it for:** Adventure and a wider sense of scale.
**Budget:** $200-$350/day.
**Stay:** 10-14 days.

### Tier 4: The Retreats

**12. Bali, Indonesia (Ubud)**
Ubud offers a richer story than the film: world-class yoga, rice paddies, temple ceremonies, and a cost of living that can stretch a trip into several weeks. The expat community of women 45+ is thriving.
**Choose it for:** Extended stays, yoga, and wellness.
**Budget:** $60-$150/day.
**Stay:** 14-30 days.

**13. Costa Rica (Nosara or Santa Teresa)**
Surf lessons at any age. Yoga every morning. Monkeys in the trees. Fresh ceviche at sunset. Safe, welcoming, English-friendly, and packed with solo women travelers.
**Choose it for:** Sun, movement, and an active week.
**Budget:** $100-$200/day.
**Stay:** 7-14 days.

**14. The Scottish Highlands**
For the introvert. Castle ruins, single-malt whisky, and landscapes shaped over centuries. Rent a cottage. Read books. Walk for hours in deep quiet. Come home knowing what silence sounds like.
**Choose it for:** Reading, solitude, and recovery after a season of caregiving.
**Budget:** $120-$250/day.
**Stay:** 5-10 days.

**15. Puglia, Italy**
Puglia brings trulli houses, olive groves, orecchiette, burrata, and Primitivo into one sun-bleached landscape. Its slow pace follows you home.
**Choose it for:** Food, small towns, and a road beyond the familiar route.
**Budget:** $100-$250/day.
**Stay:** 7-10 days.

## The Packing Principle

<Image src="/blog/travel-packing-flatlay.jpg" alt="Curated solo travel packing essentials" width={2048} height={2048} className="w-full max-w-lg mx-auto rounded-2xl my-12" />

One carry-on. Begin there. The art of solo travel packing:

- **3 bottoms** (dark jeans, comfortable pants, a skirt or shorts)
- **5 tops** (2 tees, 1 blouse, 1 sweater, 1 layer)
- **1 dress** that works for dinner
- **2 pairs of shoes** (comfortable walking shoes + sandals or flats)
- **1 scarf** (solves every styling problem and doubles as a blanket)
- **Neutral color palette** so everything mixes

Packing light makes every train, staircase, and hotel change feel easier.

## The Quiet Parts of Going Alone

**You will eat alone. It will feel strange for exactly one meal.** By the second meal, you'll have a book, a glass of wine, and the realization that this is actually wonderful.

**You will take a wrong turn.** It may lead to the best café of the trip.

**Someone will worry about you.** Let their concern travel with them. Your plan and your judgment travel with you.

**You will come home different.** The change arrives quietly. The woman who walks back through her own front door carries proof that she can handle the world on her own terms.

> The world grows richer after 50 because your reasons for seeing it get better.
